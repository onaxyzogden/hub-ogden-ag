Moontrance —
A place built around presence

A living property integrating regenerative land, Islamic-adab-conscious hospitality, and the experience of witnessing signs of the Creator.

Moontrance is not a retreat centre. It is a lived experiment in what land, community, and economy look like when they are designed from different premises.

Men's cohort development. Family life. Regenerative agriculture. Educational immersion. Hospitality rooted in adab. Each element placed with intention, phased with humility, and explained — not just designed.

The land serves the community. The community serves what lasts. The Atlas makes the vision legible.

Design principle
The tool asks 'what does this land need?' before 'what can this land produce?'. Assessments lead with ecological opportunity, not yield potential.
The program
Prayer pavilion and quiet reflection zones are first-class design elements — not optional add-ons. Dawn and sunset viewpoints are placed before structures.
The identity
This is not a generic retreat product. It is a OGDEN tool — the vision of a living property that can be built, visited, and learned from. Every feature is traceable to this identity.

◎  Land Intelligence

Atlas — For seeing land whole
and building it wisely

A land intelligence system covering 30 design domains — ecological, spiritual, economic, and generational.

The Atlas is not a drawing application. It is a land intelligence system built around the belief that land design is an act of stewardship, not control.

Seven federal public APIs auto-populated on project creation. A rule-based site assessment engine scoring across six dimensions. AI outputs that carry confidence levels, data source citations, and a 'needs site visit' flag — never authoritative.

Explainability over magic. If the Atlas can't explain why it made a recommendation, it should not make one.
