Our Purpose

We exist to help restore your relationship with your creator

"To cultivate a place of remembrance, learning, and stewardship where people encounter the beauty of God's creation."

Moontrance Collective is a values-rooted agritourism destination in Halton Hills built around a simple belief: that time spent on the land — learning, resting, and reflecting — can restore something essential in us. We bring together hospitality, education, and Islamic values in one living, breathing place.

🌿

Caring for the land as a trust, not a resource to exploit.

Our Hospitality Standard

Rooted in Islamic Values, shaped by care

I

Halal, always

From food to atmosphere, every hospitality choice is made with halal standards at the centre. You will never need to check or compromise.

II

Privacy-conscious design

Accommodations and shared spaces are designed with privacy, dignity, and family comfort in mind. Safety is built into the structure, not just the policy.

III

Prayer-friendly rhythms

The experience is paced with salah in mind. Prayer spaces, direction, and quiet time are built naturally into the schedule, not squeezed in as an afterthought.

IV

Values-safe for the whole family

Parents can relax knowing this is an environment built around the same values they bring to their homes. No filtering needed.

Common Questions

We're happy to answer anything

What kind of experiences do you offer? +
We offer guided farm and land tours, family half-day experiences, seasonal educational workshops, and overnight stays. Each experience is rooted in sustainable living, stewardship, and hospitality shaped by Islamic values. Whether you want a two-hour introduction or a full family weekend, there's a path for you.
Is the environment family-friendly? +
Yes — deeply so. Several of our experiences are specifically designed with families in mind, including child-friendly activities, values-conscious hospitality, and a setting built for parents who want to relax without compromising what they care about. Children are welcomed and included as full participants.
Is food halal? +
Our hospitality approach is rooted in Islamic values, which includes halal food standards wherever meals are offered. You will never need to ask or negotiate this. It is already built in.
Do I need any farming experience? +
Not at all. Our experiences are designed for guests at every level of familiarity — from people who have never set foot on a farm to those with a deep interest in sustainable systems. You will be guided, welcomed, and never made to feel unprepared.
Are stays private and modesty-friendly? +
Yes. Accommodations and shared spaces are designed with privacy, dignity, and guest comfort in mind from the ground up. We build privacy into the structure, not just the policy. Prayer spaces, direction, and quiet time are also integrated naturally into the stay experience.
Can schools or groups visit? +
Yes. We welcome Islamic schools, homeschool groups, family circles, educational organizations, and aligned retreat groups. Tailored visits and hosted group experiences are available. Please reach out through the contact form to discuss your group's needs.
