Moontrance is looking for founding members — people who are ready to sign a covenant, steward a piece of land, and build something that their grandchildren will inherit as Waqf.

What founding membership means

A covenant, not a contract.
Shared principles before shared land.

  1. 01

    Signing the Founding Covenant

    A written commitment to shared principles — riba-free economics, shura governance, tiered membership, and the commitment to transition the land to Waqf. The covenant is what makes you a member. Not a deposit.

  2. 02

    Participating in Shura

    Governance by consultation, with an Amir holding final authority. Founding members participate in shura — the collective discernment that shapes how the community develops, what it prioritises, and how resources are allocated.

  3. 03

    Contributing to the Shared Vision

    Whether through skills, time, capital, or connections — each founding member brings something real to the table. This is not a passive investment. It is active stewardship of a shared amana.

  4. 04

    Building for Generations

    The founding members are building something designed to outlast them. The Waqf conversion clause means the land is not an asset to be sold — it is a trust to be passed forward. Sadaqah jariyah made structural.

What this is not

Clarity protects everyone.

This is not a real estate investment. There is no expected financial return. This is not a retreat centre looking for customers. It is not a co-housing development with units to sell. It is a values-rooted community being built from covenant — and it requires people who understand that the reward is not financial. The reward is what lasts.

Reach out
