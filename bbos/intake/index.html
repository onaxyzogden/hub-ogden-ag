The intake charter exists so we can both understand what's real before anything is committed. It is not a sales call. It is a declaration — made before Ash-Shahīd, the Witness.

Scroll

What the intake asks

Six honest declarations.
No performance required.

  1. 01

    Capital

    What you actually have available — not what you hope to have. Runway, reserves, and the honest picture of your financial position.

  2. 02

    Skills

    What you can verifiably do. Not aspirations — evidence. The intake asks for proof links, not self-assessments.

  3. 03

    Proof

    Integrity proof links — portfolio, testimonials, past work, credentials. What can be shown to someone who doesn't already know you.

  4. 04

    Constraints

    Time, energy, aversions, health, obligations. The things that shape what you can actually carry — named without shame, because constraints are honest data.

  5. 05

    Geography

    Where you operate, who you can serve, what your market looks like from where you actually stand.

  6. 06

    Regulatory Pre-Check

    Licenses, certifications, insurance, legal requirements. What's in place and what's missing — flagged before it becomes a problem downstream.

Why it matters

Everything built above this
rests on the honesty of this foundation.

اللَّهُمَّ أَرِنَا الْحَقَّ حَقًّا وَارْزُقْنَا اتِّبَاعَهُ

"O Allah, show us the truth as truth and grant us the ability to follow it."

From the supplications of the Prophet ﷺ

The intake does not ask who you want to be. It asks who you are right now. A gap in the intake is not a failure — it is honest data that protects both of us from building on a foundation that cannot hold.

Ready

When you're ready,
the intake is here.

You can complete the intake in your own time. There is no deadline, no pressure, and no commitment until we both agree the foundation is sound.
